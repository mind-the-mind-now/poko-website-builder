# Our Approach

::: div {.section .box .palette-contrast-terracotta .full-bleed-bg}

## A Three-Stage Rocket

### 1st Stage

With various partners, and under the supervision of our Scientific Committee, we are working to establish a label, signifying commitment to the principles of the Declaration.

Its purpose is to mark adherence to the principle of protecting the mind, so that users know that the object or service they are using bears the mark of a commitment to the values promoted by the Declaration.

### 2nd Stage

We plan actions and initiatives that will help establish public awareness of the stakes involved in protecting the mind, such as creating an award, organizing public events, appointing ambassadors, and organizing general assemblies on digital issues.

### 3rd Stage

The stage of the Universal Declaration of the Rights of the Human Mind on a planetary scale. An admittedly ideal and visionary stage, but one that sets the course for our overall approach. This is the long-term stage, giving meaning to the word "universal" in the Declaration.

:::

## A Commitment to Protect the Human Mind

_Mind the Mind Now_ is a bottom-up approach, meaning it starts from today's technological practices and uses to express the general concern of users for a harmonious coexistence between humans and mind technologies. Its endpoint – the Universal Declaration of the Rights of the Human Mind, the third stage of the Mind the Mind Now rocket – would be proclaimed in the name of the peoples of the Earth.

The first stage is that of awareness. By obtaining the Mind the Mind Now label, stakeholders are recognized for their commitment to the values of the Declaration. As such, like the Bio label, the Mind the Mind Now label provides valuable information to the user. Like it, the Mind the Mind Now label is not a general policy statement, but a mark of positioning in favor of the consumer's long-term well-being. It is therefore in no way a substitute for state policies, nor a prefiguration of global digital governance. It is, more simply but no less essentially, a mark that producers and consumers keep the mind in mind.

Its general principle: that the mind should never be considered as a mere means to ends that do not ultimately serve its good. For us, the legitimate pursuit of economic and technical performance must not come at the expense of the mind's well-being.

Here are some examples:

- Not artificially encouraging addictions
- Not artificially encouraging virality
- Not artificially encouraging manipulation of minds (nudging, reminders, solicitations, etc.)
- Vigilance regarding unjustified automated decisions
- Not encouraging users to lose control
- Ensuring no unnecessary or unjustified servitudes are created
- Watching for loss of knowledge, skills, and deprofessionalization

The second stage is that of concrete actions that will help make the principles of the Declaration a shared policy. This is the stage of operationalizing the principles just mentioned. But we are also convinced that all the measures that can be taken at different levels of public policy can only become truly effective if they are part of the broader framework of a Universal Declaration, which gives them meaning and direction.

Hence our third stage, a visionary stage that sets the course for the whole: the Universal Declaration of the Rights of the Human Mind, and the proclamation of the human mind as the common heritage of humanity.

A visionary stage, but not unrealistic: the urgency of mental health problems, the increasingly invasive presence of connected devices in our lives, the growing distrust of users towards AI, all this converges towards the pressing need to protect the mind.

The Universal Declaration of the Rights of the Human Mind, together with the joint proclamation of the human mind as the common heritage of humanity, provides the essential ethical framework for this protection. Without this new framework, everything will remain as it is. The future of humanity will remain at the mercy of digital might makes right.

We do not have this framework. Let us create it.
